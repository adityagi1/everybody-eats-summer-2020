Takeaways:¶

  • There are similar trends in the average # of households/individuals receiving SNAP benefits and the allocated funding. They all have a very large increase since April this year. The number of people and the allocated funding have reached their highest levels since 2018, and the shape of the increases is almost like a hockey stick (please see the attached screenshots for reference). The trend also shows up in Evanston ZIP codes for the number of households/individuals receiving SNAP and the allocated funding. It means that COVID-19 has led to more people/households receiving SNAP benefits, and therefore more people with food insecurity.
  • Average # of individuals receiving SNAP in the Cook county reached Feeding America's expected 2020 food insecurity number in April and has increased above the expected number.
